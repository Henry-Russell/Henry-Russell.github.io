Carbon Fiber Skinning

Composite Manufacturing Project

Project Overview

Composite manufacturing project focused on carbon fiber skinning of performance vehicle components from a Porsche GT3 RS. Working as part of a team, this project involved preparing moulds, laying up carbon fiber using a wet layup process, and producing high-quality cosmetic and structural composite parts.

Manufacturing Process

The project involved skinning multiple exterior and aerodynamic components, including air intakes, an engine cover, wing mirrors, and aerofoil brackets. Parts were prepared through surface cleaning and release application before carbon fiber sheets were cut, oriented, and laid into moulds using a wet layup process. Resin application, consolidation, curing, trimming, and finishing were carried out with a focus on achieving consistent fibre alignment and surface quality.
